Commentary

1.a

et ecce, in vallem quandam tenebrōsam horribilemque profunditāte suā oculōs dīrigēns, conspiciō ā longē fumum quasi dē clibanō consurgentem.

And LOOK!, I, directing my eyes into a certain shady valley, horrible with respect to its depth, catch sight of smoke from a long ways away, rising as if from an oven.


1.b

notāns igitur locum, festinanter dē monte descendī; et ecce, ad montis radicēs trēs latrōnēs recenter suspensōs reperiō.

Therefore, observing the place, I descended promptly down the mountain; and LOOK!, I discover three robbers, having been recently suspended, at the roots of the mountain.


1.c

horruī illicō nimiō terrōre correptus, coepīque haesitāre et dēsperāre dē salūte, putāns quod in alicuius gigantis habitatiōnem incidissem.

Immediately, I trembled, having been snatched up with excessive terror, and I began to hesitate and to despair about my health, thinking that I had fallen into the habitat of some giant.


1.d

tamen ex necessitāte audaciam sumēns, cum etiam taederet mē vivere, ante mē progredior inveniōque domunculam ostium apertum habentem, in quā ūnam tamen cum parvulō mulierculam ad prunās sedēre conspexī.

However, out of necessity, assuming my courage, when it tired me even to live, I step forward before me and I find a little house having an open entrance/door, in which I saw moreover that one little woman was sitting with an itty-bitty person (i.e. baby) at the embers.


2.a

intrō autem domum, accedō ad eam, salutō interrogōque quid ibi sola agat, si virum habeat, quantumve ab hominum habitatiōne distem.

But I enter the house, approach to her, greet her and ask what she is doing there alone, if she has a husband, or how much I am standing apart away from the habitat of men.


2.b

illa verō, trīginta mīlibus ā terrā habitabilī mē remōtum esse affirmāns, subinfert cum lacrimīs sē cum fīliō nocte transactā ā sinū virī suī ab eīs, quās strigēs vocant, raptam fore, dēlātamque in heremum, iussam etiam ut fīlium coqueret coctumque proponeret serō strigibus dēvōrandum.

She, in truth, affirming that I was removed thirty miles from the habitable ground, adds with tears that she herself with her son when the night was finished was snatched from the lap of her husband by them whom they call witches, and was carried away into the wilderness, and also was ordered to cook her son, and to display him having been cooked, who must be devoured by the witches later.


3.a

ad haec ego, super mulieris casū motus misericordiā, fīlium cum ipsā mē liberātūrum promittō,

I, to these things, promise that I will free her son with her, having been moved with pity over the misfortune of the woman,


3.b

et quamquam lassitūdine nimiā nimiāque inediā affectus essem, quamquam etiam dē meā desperārem salūte, recucurrī tamen ad illōs trēs latrōnēs quōs pendentēs transieram;

And although I had been affected by too much exhaustion and too much hunger, and although I was also despairing about my health, however, I ran back to those three thieves, whom hanging I had passed across. (whom I found hanging)


3.c

depositumque eōrum medium, quī pinguissimus erat, attulī mulierī, monēns ut, fīliō mihi commissō, coctum latrōnem lamiīs apponeret.

I brought to the woman the middle one of them, having been placed down, who was the fattest/juiciest, advising that she should place the robber having been cooked for the witches, with her son having been entrusted to me.


4.a

cōncessit illa, fīliumque mihi tradēns, latrōnem in frusta concisum ignī superposuit.

She agreed, and handing over her son to me, placed robber, having been cut up into crumbs, over the fire.


4.b

porrō ego, parvulum optimē in concavō abscondēns lignō, mē prope domum abscondī, volēns et monstra, dum venīrent, vidēre et, si opus esset, succurrere mulierī.

I, at a distance, hiding the very small (child) very well in a hollow tree, hid myself near the house, also wanting to see the monsters, when they came, and if there is a need to help the woman.


5.a

ecce autem, sōle iam Hesperiās tingente undās, intueor dē montibus quasi quāsdam simiās innumerabilēs cum strepitū magnō descendere, nesciōquid cruentum post sē trahentēs.

But look!, with the sun now painting the Hesperian (western) waves, I see that (something like) as if countless (certain) monkeys were descending down (from) the mountains with a great high-pitched noise, dragging something bloody after/behind themselves.


5.b

quae, intrantēs domum, maximum accendunt rogum illudque cruentum inter sē dentibus carpentēs dēvorant.

The witches, entering the house, they set fire to the great fire pit, and, seizing that bloody thing, devour it with their teeth among themselves.


5.c

intervallō deinde factō, ollam nefandam ab igne deponunt frustaque coctī latrōnis inter sē dividentēs sibi Thyesteam peragunt cenam.

With an interval having been made, They place the wicked pot down from the fire, and, dividing the crumbs of the robber having been cooked among themselves, they make a Thyestean dinner for themselves.


6.a

hōc factō, illa, quae inter ceterās potentior vidēbatur, mulierem interrogat utrum eius fīlium an alium dēvōrāssent.

With this thing having been done, she, who seemed more powerful among the others, asked the woman whether they had eaten her son or another.


6.b

quā sīc fuisse factum respondente: “magis,” ait lamia, “putō quod, fīliō reservātō, ūnum illōrum trium nobis apposueris latrōnum.

With her responding THAT it had been done thus (in this way), the witch said: “I think more that, with the son having been reserved, you placed one of those three robbers for us.


6.c

quod ut citius probem, ite!” ait tribus strigibus; “deferte mihi dē quōlibet latrōnum frustum carnis!”

Go, so that I might prove that more swiftly!” she said to the three witches; bring to me a bit/slice/crumb of meat from one of the robbers.”


7.a

hoc ego audiēns festinus praecucurrī meque inter duōs latronēs medium manibus suspendō.

I, hearing this, hurrying, ran before, and I suspend myself by my hands in the middle amoung two robbers hands.


7.b

subsectae illicō strigēs duō frusta dē natibus latronum abscidunt, tertium de femore meo (ut adhuc cicatrix et fossa indicat) tollentēs, et post haec ad suam principem revertuntur.

Immediately the witches with the long gross ugly bloody don’t talk about them nails cut away two slices/crumbs down from the buttocks of the robbers, raising a third scrap/slice/crumb down from my thigh (as until now the scar and a ditch indicate), and after these things they are return to their chief.


8.a

Graviter igitur sauciātus, dē lignō, in quō pependeram, mē submittō; locumque vulneris pannīs lineīs circumligāns, sanguinem, quī instar rivulī in terram defluēbat, stagnāre voluī nec potuī.

Therefore, having been gravely wounded, I send myself down from the wood, on which I had hung; and binding the location of the wound with the linen cloths, I wanted to stop the blood, which in the image of the (like a) stream flowed onto the ground, and (but) not was able to.


8.b

Magis tamen dē salūte mulieris, quam sub fide meā defendendam susceperam, quam dē mē ipsō sollicitus ad meās rediī latebrās, crebrō ibi ob nimium sanguinis fluxum, nimiamque inediam, vigiliās, lassitudinem, defectum cordis patiēns.

However, worried more about the health of the wife – whom I had undertaken to defend under/on my honor – than about myself, I returned to my hiding places, especially suffering there because of the excessive flow of blood, and excessive hunger, and watchfulness and weariness, and failure of heart.


9.a

Illa verō lamiārum princeps singula latrōnum frusta degustāns, cum meam quoque carnem ōre cruentō temptāsset, “īte,” ait, “citius latronemque medium apportāte, quia recentes et optimae sunt carnēs eius.”

But (verō) that chief of the witches, tasting scraps of the robbers one by one, when she had tried my meat also with a bloody mouth, said: “GO quickly GET OUTTA HERE!, and (more) carry the middle robber, because his meat is fresh and the best. yum.”


9.b

hōc ego auditō, ad suspendium rediī mēque iterum inter latrōnēs suspendō. veniunt iterum ministrae tenebrārum mēque depositum ā lignō per manūs ac pedēs perque capillōs suprā veprēs ac cautēs ad domum usque prōtrahunt.

I, with this thing having been heard, returned to suspend myself (suspension) and I suspend myself among the robbers. The ministers of darkness(es) came again and they drag me, having been placed down from the wood, by my hands and feet and by my hair, above thorn bushes and pointy rocks all the way to the house.


9.c

iamque singulae super mē dentēs acuēbant iamque avida omnium ad mē devorandum hiābant ōra, cum ecce, nesciōcuius occultae virtūtis sibi contrariae perterritae maiestate, cum clamore magnō velut quaedam tempestās per ostium, per tectum perque foramina domūs diffugiunt, mē intactō cum muliere relictō.

And now, one by one, the witches were sharpening their teeth above me, and now the eager mouths of all of them were gaping to devour me, when look! they, having been scared by the majesty/wonderfulness of some hidden virtue contrary to them, with a great shout just like a certain storm, they flee in all directions through the entrance and through the roof and through the holes of the house, with me untouched and having been left behind with the woman.


10.a

statim autem post duārum vel trium horārum spatium, effugātās noctis tenebrās ministrāsque tenebrārum rutilāns diēī aurōra subsequitur.

But at once, after a space of two or three hours, the reddening dawn of day is closely following the darkness of night having been sent away and the ministers of darkness.


10.b

Et ego, muliere arreptā ac parvulō, solitūdinem pertransiēns vix tandem post quadragintā diēs, herbārum radicibus ac foliīs arbōrum interim vicitāns, ad hominēs pervēnī, mulierem et parvulum suīs reddidī.

And I, with the woman and child having been snatched up, at last passing through the wilderness hardly after forty days, meanwhile sustaining myself by (eating) roots of herbs and by leaves of trees, I came to humans, I returned the wife and child to their own (people).
