Latin Text


1.a

et ecce, in vallem quandam tenebrōsam horribilemque profunditāte suā oculōs dīrigēns, conspiciō ā longē fumum quasi dē clibanō consurgentem.

profunditate sua: Abl. of respect, describing in what way the valles is horribilis.
fumum ... consurgentem: the participle that modifies fumum contains extra information (quasi de clibano).


1.b

notāns igitur locum, festinanter dē monte descendī; et ecce, ad montis radicēs trēs latrōnēs recenter suspensōs reperiō.

ad ... radices: “at the roots” i.e. at the foot of the mountain. Note how the genitive montis is contained inside the prepositional phrase.


1.c

horruī illicō nimiō terrōre correptus, coepīque haesitāre et dēsperāre dē salūte, putāns quod in alicuius gigantis habitatiōnem incidissem.

putans quod ... incidissem: This is a corrupted form of the indirect statement putans me ... incidisse.
in ... habitationem: Again note how extra information (alicuius gigantis) is contained within the prepositional phrase.


1.d

tamen ex necessitāte audaciam sumēns, cum etiam taederet mē vivere, ante mē progredior inveniōque domunculam ostium apertum habentem, in quā ūnam tamen cum parvulō mulierculam ad prunās sedēre conspexī.

etiam: “still.” In the previous story, the speaker had already undergone many difficulties, and so continues to be very weary at this point.


2.a

intrō autem domum, accedō ad eam, salutō interrogōque quid ibi sola agat, si virum habeat, quantumve ab hominum habitatiōne distem.

interrogo: introduces three indirect questions (quid ... agat, si ... habeat, quantum ... distem)
quantum: “to what extent” or “how far”


2.b

illa verō, trīginta mīlibus ā terrā habitabilī mē remōtum esse affirmāns, subinfert cum lacrimīs sē cum fīliō nocte transactā ā sinū virī suī ab eīs, quās strigēs vocant, raptam fore, dēlātamque in heremum, iussam etiam ut fīlium coqueret coctumque proponeret serō strigibus dēvōrandum.

triginta milibus: ablative degree of difference, showing how far away they are from civilization.
subinfert: introduces a long indirect statement with three infinitives raptam fore, delatam (fore), iussam (fore).
fore = esse
iussam: introduces an indirect command with ut.
devorandum: modifies filium above. This construction depicts purpose – “to be eaten.”


3.a

ad haec ego, super mulieris casū motus misericordiā, fīlium cum ipsā mē liberātūrum promittō,

liberaturum = liberaturum esse, by ellipsis.


3.b

et quamquam lassitūdine nimiā nimiāque inediā affectus essem, quamquam etiam dē meā desperārem salūte, recucurrī tamen ad illōs trēs latrōnēs quōs pendentēs transieram;

quamquam ... quamquam ... tamen: correlative conjunctions link these three clauses together (“although..., and although..., nevertheless...”)


3.c

depositumque eōrum medium, quī pinguissimus erat, attulī mulierī, monēns ut, fīliō mihi commissō, coctum latrōnem lamiīs apponeret.

filio ... commisso: ablative absolute. mihi is an indirect object for commisso.
mulierī: dative with the compound verb attuli.


4.a

cōncessit illa, fīliumque mihi tradēns, latrōnem in frusta concisum ignī superposuit.

igni: dative with the compound verb superposuit.


4.b

porrō ego, parvulum optimē in concavō abscondēns lignō, mē prope domum abscondī, volēns et monstra, dum venīrent, vidēre et, si opus esset, succurrere mulierī.

et ... et: “both ... and”
volens: introduces the complementary infinitives videre and succurrere.
si opus esset: “if there was a need”


5.a

ecce autem, sōle iam Hesperiās tingente undās, intueor dē montibus quasi quāsdam simiās innumerabilēs cum strepitū magnō descendere, nesciōquid cruentum post sē trahentēs.

sole ... tingente undas: abl. absolute
quasi ... innumerabiles: The speaker compares the witches to monkeys as they enter the scene.
nescioquid: “something” (literally, “I know not what”)


5.b

quae, intrantēs domum, maximum accendunt rogum illudque cruentum inter sē dentibus carpentēs dēvorant.

quae: antecedent is the witches. Translate as “they.”


5.c

intervallō deinde factō, ollam nefandam ab igne deponunt frustaque coctī latrōnis inter sē dividentēs sibi Thyesteam peragunt cenam.

intervallo ... facto: “with an interval having been made”, i.e. “when an interval of time had passed.”
Thyesteam ... cenam: “Thyestean dinner,” a cannibalistic meal. In mythology, Atreus took revenge on his brother Thyestes by killing Thyestes’ two sons and serving them to their father as “people soup.”


6.a

hōc factō, illa, quae inter ceterās potentior vidēbatur, mulierem interrogat utrum eius fīlium an alium dēvōrāssent.

utrum ... an: “whether ... or.”


6.b

quā sīc fuisse factum respondente: “magis,” ait lamia, “putō quod, fīliō reservātō, ūnum illōrum trium nobis apposueris latrōnum.

quā ... respondente: Ablative absolute, containing an indirect statement within.
magis: “rather.” The head witch is calling the mother out on her lie.
puto quod...: again a corrupted form of the indirect statement puto tē apposuisse (cf. 1.c).


6.c

quod ut citius probem, ite!” ait tribus strigibus; “deferte mihi dē quōlibet latrōnum frustum carnis!”

quod: “this thing.” The antecedent is the witch’s hypothesis of what the mother actually did.


7.a

hoc ego audiēns festinus praecucurrī meque inter duōs latronēs medium manibus suspendō.


7.b

subsectae illicō strigēs duō frusta dē natibus latronum abscidunt, tertium de femore meo (ut adhuc cicatrix et fossa indicat) tollentēs, et post haec ad suam principem revertuntur.

subsectae: “pared down” as to the nails. In literature, witches are often described with nails in this way. This is relevant to the fact that the witches are about to tear apart the cadavers with their nails.
ut adhuc cicatrix et fossa indicat: at this comment, one imagines the narrator of the story pointing out his scar to his audience, offering it as proof that this story is true.


8.a

Graviter igitur sauciātus, dē lignō, in quō pependeram, mē submittō; locumque vulneris pannīs lineīs circumligāns, sanguinem, quī instar rivulī in terram defluēbat, stagnāre voluī nec potuī.

instar + gen.: “just as”
stagnare: a complementary infinitive with both volui and potui. Its direct object is sanguinem.


8.b

Magis tamen dē salūte mulieris, quam sub fide meā defendendam susceperam, quam dē mē ipsō sollicitus ad meās rediī latebrās, crebrō ibi ob nimium sanguinis fluxum, nimiamque inediam, vigiliās, lassitudinem, defectum cordis patiēns.

magis ... quam: correlative conjunctions to compare the speaker’s worry (sollicitus) about the woman and himself.
defendendam: modifies quam. This construction is again used to express purpose (cf. devorandum in 2.b).
inediam, vigilias, lassitudinem, defectum cordis: these are all direct objects of patiens.


9.a

Illa verō lamiārum princeps singula latrōnum frusta degustāns, cum meam quoque carnem ōre cruentō temptāsset, “īte,” ait, “citius latronemque medium apportāte, quia recentes et optimae sunt carnēs eius.”

temptāsset = temptāvisset (syncopated form)


9.b

hōc ego auditō, ad suspendium rediī mēque iterum inter latrōnēs suspendō. veniunt iterum ministrae tenebrārum mēque depositum ā lignō per manūs ac pedēs perque capillōs suprā veprēs ac cautēs ad domum usque prōtrahunt.

ego: placed inside the ablative absolute hoc audito, even though grammatically it belongs to suspendo.
iterum ... iterum: Note the repetition of this word to emphasize the repetition of his actions.
ministrae tenebrarum: i.e. the witches. Why is this a fitting description of them?
per manus ... capillos: describes what the witches used to drag the speaker (certainly now in pain!) back to the house.


9.c

iamque singulae super mē dentēs acuēbant iamque avida omnium ad mē devorandum hiābant ōra, cum ecce, nesciōcuius occultae virtūtis sibi contrariae perterritae maiestate, cum clamore magnō velut quaedam tempestās per ostium, per tectum perque foramina domūs diffugiunt, mē intactō cum muliere relictō.

avida: “eager;” modifies ora.
ad me devorandum: a more full version of the construction from 2.b and 8.b.
nesciocuius ... maiestate: the witches, “terrified by the appearance of some hidden virtue that was opposed to them.” The introduction of this unknown thing is a deus ex machina.
velut quaedam tempestas: The shouting witches are compared to a storm.
DEUS EX MACHINA = God from the Machine μηχανή (mechane)


10.a

statim autem post duārum vel trium horārum spatium, effugātās noctis tenebrās ministrāsque tenebrārum rutilāns diēī aurōra subsequitur.

effugatas ... tenebras ministrasque: the direct objects of subsequitur.
rutilō, -āre: to be red
effugō, -āre: to send away


10.b

Et ego, muliere arreptā ac parvulō, solitūdinem pertransiēns vix tandem post quadragintā diēs, herbārum radicibus ac foliīs arbōrum interim vicitāns, ad hominēs pervēnī, mulierem et parvulum suīs reddidī.

suīs: imply “relatives.” The mother and infant are now with their family members (and lived happily ever after?).
